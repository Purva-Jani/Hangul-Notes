A fun little irregular: ㅅ

This notorious letter, typically pronounced as "s," becomes an "sh" sound when before any "ee" sound

Examples

  • 시 → "shee"
  • 샤 → "shyah"
  • 셔 → "shyuh"
  • 쇼 → "shyoh"
  • 슈 → "shyoo"
  • 셰 → "shyeh"
  • 섀 → "shyeh"

DOUBLE & STRONG CONSONANTS

Double consonants are exactly how they sound. They're doubled.

  • ㄲ → "k"            ㅆ → "ss"
  • ㄸ → "t" or "d"        ㅉ → "ch"
  • ㅃ → "p"

Strong consonants are more built different

  • ㅋ → "kh"           ㅍ → "ph"
  • ㅌ → "th"           ㅊ → "chh"

Pronouncatissue (hold in front of mouth):

  • For regular consonants, the tissue should move a little
  • Fro double consonants, the tissue should not move
  • For strong consonants, the tissue should move a lot
